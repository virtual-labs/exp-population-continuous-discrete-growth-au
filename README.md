### About the Experiment 

The objective of the "Population with Continuous and Discrete Growth" experiment is to understand and analyze the dynamics of population growth under two distinct mathematical models: continuous growth and discrete growth. The experiment aims to demonstrate how populations evolve over time based on different growth rates and environmental factors. By comparing continuous exponential growth and discrete growth with periodic reproduction, the experiment allows users to visualize and quantify the differences in growth patterns, and explore their implications for ecological studies, resource management, and species conservation. Additionally, it provides insight into the mathematical modeling techniques that can be applied to real-world population dynamics.
